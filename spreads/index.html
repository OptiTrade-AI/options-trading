Option Spreads for Small Accounts

Harness defined-risk spreads to grow with confidence

Introduction

Small accounts can trade safely with defined-risk spreads, where your maximum loss is known upfront.

You’ll learn:

  • Put Credit Spreads (neutral to bullish)
  • Bull Call Spreads (bullish)
  • How to choose strikes and manage risk
  • Practical brokerage examples

Put Credit Spread

What Is It?

A neutral-to-bullish strategy. You sell a put option at a higher strike price and buy a put at a lower strike price (same expiration). You collect a net credit upfront.

Why Use It?

  • Limited Risk: Your maximum loss is capped.
  • Low Capital: Requires 80-90% less cash than selling a put alone.
  • High Win Rate: 60–75% chance of profit with proper strike selection.*

How It Works

Metric Formula
Max Profit Credit Received × 100
Max Loss (Strike Width – Credit) × 100
Break-even Short Strike – Credit

*Based on one contract (100 shares). Win rate assumes deltas of 15–40.

Example

Stock Price: $52

Trade:

  • Sell 1 × $50 put for $1.50
  • Buy 1 × $45 put for $0.50

Net Credit: $1.00

Outcome Value
Max Profit $100
Max Loss $400
Break-even $49.00

Bull Call Spread

What Is It?

A bullish strategy. You buy a call option at a lower strike price and sell a call at a higher strike price (same expiration). You pay a net debit upfront.

Why Use It?

  • Limited Risk: Your loss is capped at the debit paid.
  • Affordable: 40-60% cheaper than buying a call alone.
  • Targeted Gains: Profits from moderate price increases.

How It Works

Metric Formula
Max Profit (Strike Width – Debit) × 100
Max Loss Debit Paid × 100
Break-even Long Strike + Debit

Example

Stock Price: $52

Trade:

  • Buy 1 × $50 call for $3.00
  • Sell 1 × $55 call for $1.00

Net Debit: $2.00

Outcome Value
Max Profit $300
Max Loss $200
Break-even $52.00

Risk Management & Position Sizing

  • 2–5% Rule: Risk only 2–5% of your account per trade.
  • Exit Early: Close trades at 50–80% of max profit or 21 days before expiration.
  • Diversify: Avoid multiple trades on similar assets to reduce risk.

Key Takeaways

  • Defined-risk spreads keep losses manageable for small accounts.
  • Put credit spreads work best when stocks stay stable or rise.
  • Bull call spreads profit from moderate upward moves.
  • Focus on small, consistent gains to grow your account safely.
