What Are Options?

Options are contracts giving you the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a set price on or before expiration.

Key Point: One option contract typically controls 100 shares of the underlying stock.

Analogy: Like a restaurant reservation—you secure the table, but you don't have to show up.

Buying vs. Selling Calls and Puts

Four fundamental strategies: buyers pay a premium, sellers receive a premium and accept an obligation.

Calls

Buy Call

  • Right to buy 100 shares at the strike price
  • Premium paid upfront
  • Profits when stock price exceeds strike price plus premium
  • Maximum loss is the premium paid; profit potential is unlimited

Sell Call

  • Obligation to sell 100 shares at the strike price if exercised
  • Premium received upfront
  • Profit limited to premium received; risk is potentially unlimited

Puts

Buy Put

  • Right to sell 100 shares at the strike price
  • Premium paid upfront
  • Profits when stock price falls below strike price minus premium
  • Maximum loss is the premium paid; profit potential is strike price minus premium

Sell Put

  • Obligation to buy 100 shares at the strike price if exercised
  • Premium received upfront
  • Profit limited to premium received; risk is strike price minus premium

Call Options Explained

You get the right to buy shares at a set strike price. Pay a premium for this right.

Example: XYZ at $100. Buy $105 call for $3 premium.

  • If XYZ price goes above $108, profit equals the difference between stock price and (strike price plus premium)
  • If XYZ price stays at or below $108, loss is the premium paid ($3)

Put Options Explained

You get the right to sell shares at a set strike price. Pay a premium for this right.

Example: XYZ at $100. Buy $95 put for $2 premium.

  • If XYZ price falls below $93, profit equals the difference between strike price and stock price minus premium
  • If XYZ price stays at or above $93, loss is the premium paid ($2)

Options Terminology

  • Strike Price: Price at which you can buy (call) or sell (put).
  • Premium: Cost of the option contract.
  • Expiration Date: When the option expires.
  • In‑the‑Money (ITM): Call: stock above strike; Put: stock below strike.
  • Out‑of‑the‑Money (OTM): Call: stock below strike; Put: stock above strike.
  • At‑the‑Money (ATM): Stock price is roughly equal to strike price.

Basic Options Math

  • Call Break-Even: Strike price plus premium.
  • Put Break-Even: Strike price minus premium.
  • Max Profit: Call: unlimited; Put: strike price minus premium.
  • Max Risk: Premium paid.
